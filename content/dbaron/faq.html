Who are you... ?

I'm Doug Baron, a sharp Silicon Valley veteran who loves to build software -- and is exceptionally good at it.  I'm a fast learner and have taught myself countless skills along the way to producing outstanding, end-user production code, both at small companies and as an independent consultant.

I'm a strong Internet infrastructure and application developer, focused primarily on Java and XML technologies including Struts, Tiles, JSP and Taglib; DOM, XSchema and XSLT.

What kind of experience do you have?

My work in the Valley began with Living Videotext (LVT), makers of ThinkTank. I was Lead Developer of MORE for the Macintosh, the successor to ThinkTank 512. After LVT's merger with Symantec and the release of MORE 3.0 (which I managed), I joined UserLand Software, developing Frontier, the first system level scripting system for the Macintosh.

In recent years I have worked at internet startups Corio, Inc. and Webalo, Inc., developing Internet infrastructure and applications in Java, working in Unix environments (Sun Solaris and RedHat Linux), using source code control systems CVS and Visual SourceSafe. In between startups, I've worked as an independent consultant, developing Web applications in the Windows environment (2000 Advanced Server) using Microsoft SQL Server, JavaScript, ASP and Cold Fusion.

My full experience is detailed in my resume.

What did you do most recently?

A few months back I completed a project at Cirrus Logic, developing a Struts/Tiles/JSP - based Web application running under Tomcat, building on a proprietary XML content management system (CMS), and leveraging struts-html, struts-beans, struts-logic and struts-tiles tag libraries.

Another recent project for Aquaminds, Inc. was developing an XSLT-based export library.

April though September of '01 I worked at Webalo, Inc., a very small startup in the wireless arena, developing an internet service platform in Java. In the process, I learned XSLT and VXML, both of which are pretty cool and fun; I'd love to do more work with them. At Webalo, I also became more expert at the Swing libraries.

What's the best thing you've done?

Well, it's hard to pick one thing. I really enjoyed and am still proud of the work I did on MORE, which ended up on the desktop of thousands of users who gave it rave reviews. Frontier also had a great fan club, and interacting with those users online and working their feedback into subsequent releases was gratifying.

Must of the Web work I've done of late has been interesting, challenging and fun, including the Struts web application prototype I built for Cirrus Logic, and the server management portal I developed for FocusEngine a few years back. I look forward to continuing working with Java- and XML-based technologies to harnessing the web and reach more people with the applications I develop in the future.

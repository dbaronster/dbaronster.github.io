PASSIONADA is a story of second chances. Celia Amonte (Sophia Milos) is a beautiful Portuguese American woman who cannot move on from the loss of her husband, a young fisherman who died at sea. She and her daughter Vicky (Emmy Rossum) live with her mother in law (Lupe Ontiveros) in the seaside fishing community of New Bedford, Massachusetts. Celia works as a seamstress during the day, but at night she sings the beautiful Fado music that she learned from her Portuguese mother. Celia’s passion and sadness are expressed deeply in this soulful music.

Celia and her daughter Vicky are close, but Vicky has a secret. She is making excursions to the local gambling casino. There she meets Charlie Beck (Jason Isaacs), a charming Englishman and professional gambler. Vicky wants Charlie to help her learn to gamble, but he declines.

When Charlie and his friends (Teresa Russell and Seymour Cassel) visit the restaurant where Celia is performing, Charlie is thunderstruck by Celia’s sensual beauty and the passion of her music. But Celia is not open to Charlie’s charm.

When Vicky discovers Charlie’s dilemma, she makes him an interesting proposition. If he will teach her how to count cards at blackjack, she’ll help him get a date with her mother.

Slowly Charlie wins Celia’s heart. He makes Celia laugh and gradually he reawakens her passion for life and love. But to get close to her, Charlie has lied about who he is. And Celia finds out the truth before he can tell her.

Charlie and Celia must each face their pasts and move on, in order to open their hearts and become each other’s “passionada”. PASSIONADA is a story of hope and of second chances, with a happy ending truly worth celebrating.
